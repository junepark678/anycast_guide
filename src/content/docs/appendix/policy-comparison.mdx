# RIR Policy Comparison

This appendix contains a general comparison of key policies outlined in their corresponding public documents by the five Regional Internet Registries (RIRs): ARIN, RIPE NCC, APNIC, LACNIC, and AFRINIC. This comparison is not exhaustive and is intended for informational purposes only. For the most accurate and up-to-date information, please refer to the official RIR policy documents. This comparison is not a substitute for legal or professional advice.

## General Provisions

### ARIN

Most policies are outlined in the [Number Resource Policy Manual (NRPM)](https://www.arin.net/participate/policy/nrpm/).

### RIPE NCC

Policies are documented in the [RIPE Document Store](https://www.ripe.net/publications/docs/).

### APNIC

Policies are available in the [APNIC Internet Number Resource Policies](https://www.apnic.net/community/policy/resources).

### LACNIC

Policies are available in the [LACNIC Policy Manual](https://www.lacnic.net/680/2/lacnic/).

Note: LACNIC requires contacting an NIR if available in your country (Brazil/Mexico).

### AFRINIC

Policies are available in the [AFRINIC Consolidated Policy Manual](https://afrinic.net/policy/manual).

Note: AFRINIC has one of the strictest presence requirements, along with the lack of outbound IPv4 inter-RIR transfers (see section 5 of the CPM).

## Fees and Membership

### ARIN

Outlined in the [ARIN Fee Schedule](https://www.arin.net/resources/fees/fee_schedule/).

Generally, the annual fees range from 275 USD for /40 total IPv6 space and 3 ASNs, up to 282,240 USD for networks with more than a /4 of IPv6. (Note: the largest IPv6 allocation made in ARIN, as of January 2026, is a /16 to Capital One, 2630::/16)
